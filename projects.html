    SustainGym


    Despite the many successes of reinforcement learning (RL), reliable performance in real-world scenarios remains a unique challenge. To remedy this issue, researchers must benchmark RL algorithms on real world settings. Unfortunately, most RL algorithms are tested on toy environements (e.g. OpenAI Gym and Petting Zoo) which fail to represent real-world conditions. Furthermore, among the few environments simulating realistic scenarios, an even fewer number solve sustainability applications. This project aims to solve both problems by creating a test bed of realistic sustainability applications, called SustainGym. Currently, SustainGym contains two environments: EVChargingEnv and ElectricityMarketEnv. My contribution is the implementation of ElectricityMarketEnv, an RL environment simulating a carbon conscious battery storage system in a real-time 5 minute interval electricity market.

    Tank Trouble Game in C#


    For computer programmers, collaborating on a single project with a deadline presents certain challenges. For example, initial design choices and distribution of labor must be discussed in an effective manner and a consensus reached. In the CS 3 course at Caltech, called "Introduction to Software Design," three classmates and I were tasked with creating a physics-based game in the C# programming language. We decided to create the single and multiplayer game "Tank Trouble" which involves player-controlled tanks destroying each other. My main contribution to the project was the implementation of the powerup portion of the game.

    Educational Neural Network Website


    Piquing students' interest in new technologies remains a major challenge in K-12 STEM education. One example of a technology recently gaining traction is deep neural networks (DNNs). A form of effective bio-inspired computing, DNNs are being used in a wide range of industries. Unfortunately, the technology's basis in complex mathematical concepts can be intimidating. My project, as a part of Notre Dame's Research Experience for Teachers (RET) program, was to create an open-source website that allows students to tinker with hyperparameters and observe the corresponding learning process on the MNIST dataset.
